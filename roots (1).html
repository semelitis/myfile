Roots and Rot — Biography

I am made of seasons.

Each one a shedding — of a self I thought I was, of a world I thought was permanent. The garden does not mourn what falls. It simply prepares the soil for what comes next.

I work between bodies and screens, between the sacred and the disposable, between Mediterranean memory and digital futures. My practice is immersive — not because I want to overwhelm, but because I believe transformation requires full presence.

I have lived in Greece, in Catalonia, in India, in the space between cultures where belonging is always a question. This displacement became my method. The seasons became my archive.

What I was is compost.

What I am is still blooming.

What I will be — I am making now.
